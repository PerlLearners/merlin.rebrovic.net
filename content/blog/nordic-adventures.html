Although I could fill a book with photos and tales of our adventures, I'm not sure how to do a good summary, so I'll just share a couple of interesting moments and motives. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_central_norway.jpg", "Snowy central Norway.") }} {% endmark %} This is not what I expected at 1200m in central Norway in July. I was hoping for cooler temperatures than in south Europe, but this was a surprise. Luckily for us, all of this was observed from inside a train on a scenic Bergen Line from Oslo. On the way we stopped to see some fjords. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_norway_fjord.jpg", "A fjord from a boat.") }} Photos don't do fjords justice. It's simply impossible to show how huge and impressive they are. Cliffs rise from the sea hundreds of meters tall and some even cross one thousand mark, often with snowy caps. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_norway_viking_ship.jpg", "Me behind a viking ship.") }} Scandinavian naval history is old and rich, and has shaped the world as we know it many times. Viking ships are probably the most recognizable historic artifacts and are frequent motives in these lands. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_viking_ship_decoration.jpg", "Arhitectural viking ship decorations on a column.") }} [Fram][1] is a monumental ship in Norway's history. It was specially designed for arctic conditions at the end of the nineteenth century and used for expeditions on the Arctic and Antarctic regions. It also carried the crew that first reached the South Pole in 1911. {{ macros.external_image("2015-07-19_fram.jpg", "Fram's bow in a museum.") }} Seeing boats in Denmark isn't anything novel by now, but what's behind them is more interesting. It's Sunday night and the streets are bustling with activity. A relaxed and balanced approach to life is just one factor why Denmark is consistently rated as one of the happiest countries in the world. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_denmark_harbor.jpg", "A historical harbor in Copenhagen.") }} {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_denmark_restaurant.jpg", "A restaurant in a historical harbor in Copenhagen.") }} Smaller cities and villages still offer a glimpse of the past. One just needs to turn from the main street and experience a different time. {{ macros.external_wide_image("2015-07-19_old_city_street.jpg", "An old city street in Bergen.") }} The old Swedish ship called [Vasa][3] is what we would call an epic fail, but with a happy ending. Built in the first half of the seventeenth century, it managed to sail less than a mile after launch and then immediately sank due to bad design. It was salvaged in the 1960s and turned into a museum. Because it's 98% preserved, it serves as an enormous cultural and historical heritage.
